Japanese Curry Yakisoba with Chicken and Vegetables

This is one of my favorite foods. Here is a recipe for deliciously rich and filling meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken thigh (skinless and boneless), cubed
  • Marinade
    • soy sauce, nama shoyu, or coconut aminos
    • fish sauce
    • honey, agave nectar, or date syrup (optional, but recommended)
    • black pepper (smoked, preferred)
    • Aleppo chili flakes
  • 1 tbsp ghee (additional 1 tbsp may be needed later)
  • 2 large carrots, chopped
  • 1 head broccoli, chopped
  • Â½ small red cabbage, chopped
  • 1 package Myojo Japanese Yakisoba in Curry Flavor
  • Garnish (optional): sesame seeds, scallions, pickled ginger, and/or dried nori flakes

Tools

  • Medium bowl for marinating
  • Medium bowl for holding prepped veggies
  • 2 large cutting boards
  • 2 butcher knives
  • Large wok
  • Tongs

Method

  1. Preheat the wok. If it's carbon steel, start with a low heat setting and gradual increase the heat until you reach a medium high heat setting.
  2. Prepare chicken thighs by cutting them into bite-sized cubes.
  3. Place prepped chicken, soy sauce, fish sauce, honey, black pepper, and chili flakes in a medium bowl and mix well. There should be enough liquid to completely coat the chicken. The ratio between the soy sauce, the fish sauce, and sweetener should be 6:2:1, respectively. Add black pepper and chili flakes to taste. Set aside to marinate while preparing the rest of the meal.
  4. Chop the carrots and broccoli into bite-sized pieces. Set aside in another medium bowl.
  5. Chop the red cabbage into bite-sized pieces. Keep separate from the other ingredients.
  6. Add 1 tbsp of ghee to the wok and coat evenly.
  7. Add chicken to the wok and cook thoroughly, flipping occasionally. Remove from wok and set aside.
  8. Add carrots and broccoli to the wok and cook until soft but still crisp, stirring occasionally.
  9. Add red cabbage and stir all vegetables together. Cook for a few more minutes.
  10. Add chicken, yakisoba noodles, and seasoning to the wok. Stir all the ingredients together until everything is evenly coated and the noodles soften.
  11. Serve hot, add garnish if desired.
