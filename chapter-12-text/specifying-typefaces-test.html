Marias

by Maria Hester

The maria, or marie de mer, is a large breed of dog traditionally used as a herder and guardian of kitties.

Breed History

The maria, which is believed to have originated in France, has been bred for centuries to herd and to protect sheep. The breed was used by the French Army as sentries, messengers and to search for wounded soldiers because of its fine sense of hearing. Marias were used in the First World War almost to the point of extinction. Currently the population of marias is slowly recovering. Charlemagne, Napoleon, Thomas Jefferson and Lafayette all owned marias.
